## The Problem

30% of food produced is lost or wasted (1.3 billion tons/year). Cold chain infrastructure is missing in 50% of developing countries. Without refrigeration, perishable food lasts 1-2 days. With cold chain, 2-4 weeks. But cold chains cost $100B/year to maintain.

## The Solution

Superconducting coils generate magnetic fields that slow microbial growth and enzymatic reactions in food. The field reduces metabolic rates of spoilage organisms by 90%.

**How it works:**
1. Superconducting coils installed in storage container
2. Magnetic field applied at spoilage-suppressing frequency
3. Microbial metabolism slowed 90%
4. Enzymatic reactions reduced
5. Food preserved for 2-4 weeks
6. No refrigeration needed

## The Problem

Post-harvest losses are 20-40% in developing countries (30 million tons of grain/year). Storage requires fumigation (phosphine, methyl bromide) — hazardous to humans. Grain weevils develop resistance to fumigants. Mold produces aflatoxins (carcinogenic).

## The Solution

Superconducting coils generate magnetic fields that suppress insect reproduction and mold growth. The field disrupts insect egg development and fungal spore germination. Grain remains viable for 5+ years.

**How it works:**
1. Superconducting coils installed in storage facility
2. Magnetic field permeates grain mass
3. Insect egg development suppressed
4. Mold spore germination inhibited
5. Grain preserved for 5+ years
6. No chemicals, no fumigation

**Components:**
- RTS coil array (storage facility installation)
- Power supply (grid or solar)
- Temperature/humidity sensors
- Grain quality monitor
- Ventilation control

**Novel contribution:** Magnetic suppression of grain pests is demonstrated in lab settings. RTS enables continuous field deployment. No resistance development, no chemical residues.
